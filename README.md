## 📌 Project Overview

An end-to-end analytics project deconstructing YouTube's ranking algorithm through data — combining ETL pipeline documentation with a fully interactive Power BI dashboard. The project examines what signals (watch time, engagement rate, upload frequency, click-through rate, and more) actually correlate with ranking performance, and visualizes those relationships for content strategy decisions.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Content creators and marketing teams invest heavily in YouTube without a clear picture of what actually drives algorithmic visibility. This project answers: *which performance signals matter most, how do they interact, and what does that mean for content strategy?*

---

## 🔍 Analysis Approach

- ETL pipeline designed and documented: raw data extraction, transformation, and loading into Power BI
- Key ranking signals identified and mapped to measurable metrics
- Correlation analysis between engagement signals and ranking outcomes
- DAX measures built for calculated KPIs across time windows
- Dashboard designed for both strategic overview and drill-down by channel/content type

## 💡 Key Findings

- Watch time and click-through rate showed strongest correlation with ranking
- Upload consistency outperformed individual viral spikes for sustained ranking
- Engagement velocity in first 48 hours had outsized algorithmic impact
- Recommendations structured around content calendar and thumbnail optimization
